# Grant Application Checklist

## Before You Submit (30 min total)

### Step 1: Fill in Your Details (5 min)
- [ ] Open `GRANT_APPLICATION.md`
- [ ] Replace `[YOUR NAME]` with your name
- [ ] Replace `[YOUR BACKGROUND]` with 1-2 sentences
- [ ] Replace `[YOUR TELEGRAM]` with your handle
- [ ] Replace `[YOUR EMAIL]` with your email
- [ ] Replace `[YOUR GITHUB]` with your GitHub URL

### Step 2: Get Your Metrics (5 min)
- [ ] Check your bot's user count (or estimate)
- [ ] Check notarization count (or estimate)
- [ ] Replace `[INSERT]` with actual numbers
- [ ] If numbers are low, be honest - "10 users, just launched"

### Step 3: Record Demo Video (10 min)
- [ ] Go to https://loom.com (free)
- [ ] Record 2-3 min showing:
  - Send /start to @NotaryTON_bot
  - Show the payment options
  - Notarize a sample contract address
  - Show the verification response
  - Quick look at notaryton.com dashboard
- [ ] Copy the Loom link
- [ ] Replace `[LOOM LINK]` in documents

### Step 4: Update Email Pitch (5 min)
- [ ] Open `GRANT_EMAIL_PITCH.md`
- [ ] Fill in your metrics
- [ ] Add your Loom link
- [ ] Add your contact info

### Step 5: Submit (5 min)

**Option A: TON Grants Portal**
1. Go to https://ton.org/grants
2. Click "Apply for Grant"
3. Copy/paste sections from `GRANT_APPLICATION.md`
4. Attach your Loom video link
5. Submit

**Option B: Direct Email**
1. Copy content from `GRANT_EMAIL_PITCH.md`
2. Send to grants@ton.org
3. Attach full `GRANT_APPLICATION.md` as PDF

## After Submitting

### Week 1
- [ ] Join TON Dev chat: https://t.me/tondev_eng
- [ ] Introduce yourself and NotaryTON
- [ ] Ask if anyone knows grant reviewers

### Week 2
- [ ] If no response, send polite follow-up email
- [ ] Share project on Twitter with #TON
- [ ] Keep building - don't just wait

### Week 3-4
- [ ] Follow up again if needed
- [ ] Ask for feedback even if rejected
- [ ] Apply to other grants (Gitcoin, etc.)

---

## Quick Tips

1. **Be specific** - "3 launchpad integrations" not "get partners"
2. **Show don't tell** - Demo video > descriptions
3. **Modest ask** - $10K is easier to approve than $50K
4. **Milestone-based** - Shows you're accountable
5. **Follow up** - Polite persistence works
